How to Contact Brands for UGC (With a Sample Message That Works)

Published September 5, 2025 by Ebun's Vault

So, you’ve started creating UGC — maybe some unboxing videos, lifestyle shots, or reviews. But then the big question hits:

👉 “How do I actually get brands to notice me?”

Here’s the good news: brands are always looking for fresh, authentic voices. The challenge isn’t if they want you — it’s how you approach them.

Step 1: Find the Right Contact

  • Look for “PR” or “Marketing” emails on the brand’s website.
  • Check their Instagram bio or Facebook page for collab info.
  • Some brands have application portals for product testers.
  • In Africa, a few even provide WhatsApp business numbers for direct contact.

Step 2: Prepare Your Portfolio (Even if You’re New)

  • A few clear videos or photos you’ve created with everyday products is enough.
  • Post them on TikTok, Instagram, or YouTube Shorts.
  • This shows brands that you can already create UGC.

Step 3: Send a Simple, Professional Message

Keep your message short, friendly, and direct. Introduce yourself, share your portfolio, and express your interest in collaborating.

Step 4: Follow Up (But Don’t Spam)

  • If you don’t hear back after 7–10 days, send a gentle follow-up message.
  • Keep it polite.
  • Reaffirm your interest.
  • Show patience — sometimes brands are slow to reply.

🚨 Here’s the Catch…

  • While this sample message works, the hardest part is knowing:
  • Which brands actually accept UGC testers in Africa.
  • Which ones reply through email vs. WhatsApp.
  • What numbers and websites to use so your message doesn’t get lost.
