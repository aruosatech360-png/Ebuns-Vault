CV Template for Students

What is a CV?

A Curriculum Vitae (CV) is a short document that summarizes your personal details, education, skills, and any experience you have. Think of it as your professional introduction on paper. Its main purpose is to make a great first impression and secure you an interview.

Common Misconceptions About CVs

  • "I need work experience to have a CV." Not true! For students, a CV can highlight school projects, volunteer work, skills, and academic achievements. It's about showing your potential.
  • "A longer CV is always better." False. For a student, a one-page CV is perfect. Recruiters appreciate concise, relevant information that is easy to read.
  • "One CV fits all jobs." It's much more effective to tailor your CV for each specific role you apply for, highlighting the skills and experiences that are most relevant to that job.

🧾 SAMPLE STUDENT CV TEMPLATE

[Your Full Name]

📞 Phone: [Your Phone Number]

📧 Email: [Your Email Address]

🌍 Location: [Your City/State]

Personal Statement

A short paragraph about who you are, your strengths, and your career goals. Example: "I’m a motivated and enthusiastic student with strong communication and computer skills. I enjoy learning new things and working in a team. I’m eager to gain experience and contribute positively to any organization I join."

Education

[Name of School or Institution] – [Location]

📅 [Start Date] – [End Date or Present]

Qualification: [Example: SSCE, OND, B.Sc in Computer Science (in view)]

Notable Achievements (if any): [Example: Best ICT Student 2023, Member of Coding Club]

💼 Work Experience (if any)

[Job Title or Role] – [Company or Organization Name], [Location]

📅 [Start Date] – [End Date]

  • [Describe what you did or learned in short bullet points]
  • [Example: Helped with computer setup for classes and assisted younger students with basic ICT tasks]

(If you don’t have work experience yet, you can list volunteer work, school projects, or group assignments that show responsibility or teamwork.)

Skills

  • Basic computer skills (Microsoft Word, Excel, PowerPoint)
  • Strong communication and teamwork skills
  • Ability to learn quickly
  • Problem-solving and time management
  • [Add any other skills you have: typing, design, coding, social media management, etc.]

Hobbies & Interests

  • Reading tech blogs and learning new digital skills
  • Volunteering and community work
  • Creative writing or graphic design
  • Sports and teamwork activities

👥 References

Available on request.

✅ Tips Before Sending Your CV:

  • Save it as “YourName_CV.pdf” before emailing (e.g., EbunAde_CV.pdf).
  • Double-check your spelling and grammar.
  • Use a simple, clean font (like Arial or Calibri).
  • Keep it 1 page long if you’re a student.
