How to Start Writing Your Way to Success (With Examples)

Published October 29, 2025 by Ebun's Vault

1. Get a Journal

Don’t rely on random sticky notes or your phone’s notes app. You need a dedicated space—a physical or digital journal that becomes your “success command center.”

Example: Buy a simple hardcover journal. Label the first page: My Ambition Journal. Every time you open it, your brain knows, this is where I plan my future.

2. Dump Your Thoughts

Start by unloading everything in your head onto paper. Don’t judge it, don’t edit it—just write. This clears mental clutter.

Example:

Instead of holding this in your head:

  • “I want to start a business.”
  • “I should go to the gym.”
  • “I need to save money.”

Write it down like this:

  • Business idea: Sell natural skincare products.
  • Health goal: Join a gym twice a week.
  • Financial target: Save ₦50,000 by December.

Now those vague thoughts become visible and concrete.

3. Set Clear Goals

Break your ambitions into small, realistic, and measurable steps.

Example:

Instead of writing: “I want to be rich,”
Write:

  • By the end of this month, I will earn an extra ₦20,000 through side hustles.
  • By the end of this year, I will increase my savings by 15%.

Instead of writing: “I want to be healthy,”
Write:

  • I will drink 2 liters of water every day.
  • I will run 3 times per week for 20 minutes.

4. Track Your Progress

Your journal should not just hold goals; it should track whether you’re moving forward or not.

Example:

  • Week 1 Health Journal Entry: Drank water every day. Went running twice instead of three times.
  • Week 2 Plan: Adjust my running schedule to mornings before work, so I don’t miss sessions.

Now, your journal becomes a mirror—you can’t lie to yourself about what you did or didn’t do.

5. Reflect Regularly

Take time weekly or monthly to look back at what you’ve written. Reflection gives clarity and motivation.

Example:

End-of-month reflection:

  • Achieved savings goal.
  • Business idea needs more research before launching.
  • Health improved—lost 2kg.

This reflection gives you direction for the next month: double down on what’s working, adjust what isn’t.

👉 The bottom line: Writing turns ambition into strategy, and strategy into success. Without writing, your ambition is just wishful thinking.
